UC Transfer Admission Guarantee (TAG) for students applying for fall 2027 or spring 2028 admission
Six University of California (UC) campuses offer guaranteed admission to students from all California community colleges (CCC). Interested students
must meet both the shared TAG criteria as well as campus-specific requirements to qualify for a TAG. By participating in a TAG program, students can ensure
their admission to a specific UC campus. Also, students interested in pursuing the Pathways+ transfer option will use a TAG to secure their guarantee.
Shared TAG criteria
To qualify for a fall 2027 TAG at any one of the six participating campuses, students must meet the following criteria:
1. Must have completed at least 30 semester/45 quarter UC-transferable units at the time of TAG submission (end of summer 2026);
2. Must complete at least one UC-E (English) and one UC-M (Math) by fall 2026;
3. Must complete the remaining 7-course pattern by spring 2026;
4. Must complete 60 semester/90 quarter UC-transferable units (junior standing) by the end of spring 2027;
5. Must complete at least 30 semester/45 quarter UC-transferable units at one or more CCC by spring 2027, and a CCC must be the last school of
attendance in a regular session;
6. Must require no more than 19 UC-transferable units (semester or quarter) in spring 2027 (or in the last regular term) to reach junior standing;
7. Must not exceed 80 semester/120 quarter total UC-transferable units, after applying lower-division limits (students with only CCC coursework and
AP/IB/A level credits will not exceed this limit);
8. Must earn no grade lower than C in the current fall, winter and/or spring terms and remain in good academic standing;
9. Must submit a TAG application through the UC Transfer Admission Planner (UC TAP) between September 1 and September 30, 2026;
10. Must submit the UC application between October 1 and November 30, 2026, and the TAG major and application major for the TAG campus must match;
11. Must submit a Transfer Academic Update (TAU) by January 31, 2027; and,
12. Must meet all campus-specific TAG criteria outlined in the chart below.
Not eligible for TAG
The following students are not eligible for a TAG at any campus:
� Students who have already earned a bachelor�s or higher degree;
� Students previously enrolled at a UC campus who intend to return to the same UC campus;
� Students previously enrolled at a UC campus who were not in good standing when they left that UC; and,
� Students concurrently enrolled in high school at the time of TAG submission.
Please note: Transfer students do not have to participate in TAG to be considered for admission. In fact, approximately two-thirds of transfer students
admitted to UC do not have a TAG. Non-TAG students simply apply for UC admission during the appropriate application filing period. Transfer students are
responsible for accurately reporting all college coursework on their TAG and UC applications.
